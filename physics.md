## Acceleration

Change of velocity per second.

**Unit**: `m/s²`

> **Formula**: `Change of velocity / Time`

## Force

A push or a pull on an object because of an interaction with another object.

**SI Unit**: `kgm/s²` or `N`

**Quantity**: Vector

---

### Resultant force

If two forces are **parallel**, add/subtract.

If two forces are at angles, draw a to-scale tail to head diagram.

## Newton's Second Law

If resultant forces is not zero, the object will move with an acceleration.

**Unit**: `kgm/s²`

> **Formula**: `ΣF = ma`
>
> `ΣF` = Resultant Force (kgm/s²)
>
> `m` = Mass (kg)
>
> `a` = Acceleration (m/s²)

## Gravitational Force

Force between two masses.

**Unit**: `kgm/s²`

> **Formula**: `W = mg`
>
> `W` = Weight (N)
>
> `m` = Mass (kg)
>
> `g` = Gravity (m/s²)

## Friction Force

Force opposite movement occurs when 2 surfaces slide across with each other.

### Max Static Friction

**Unit**: `N`

> **Formula**: `fsmax = µsN`
>
> `fsmax` = Maximum static friction
>
> `µs` = Coefficient of static friction
>
> `N` = Normal Force

## Kinetic Friction

Occurs when the object is moving.

**Unit**: `N`

> **Formula**: `fk = µkN`
>
> `fk` = Kinetic friction
>
> `µk` = Coefficient of kinetic friction
>
> `N` = Normal Force

## Moments

Moments is the force that causes objects to rotate.

**Unit:** `Nm`

> **Formula**: `M = Fd`
>
> `M` = Moment (Nm)
>
> `F` = Force (N)
>
> `d` = Distance (m)

## Work and Energy

### Work

Work done is force that makes an object move.

**Unit**: `J` or `Nm`

> **Formula**: `W = Fd`
>
> `W` = Work (**J**, Nm)
>
> `F` = Force (N)
>
> `d` = Distance (m)

---

### Power

Power is the rate of work used/given per second.

**Unit**: `W`

> **Formula**: `P = W/t` or `P = Fd/t`
>
> `P` = Power (W)
>
> `W` = Work (J)
>
> `t` = Time (s)

---

### Kinetic Energy

Kinetic Energy comes with **movement**.

**Unit**: `J`

> **Formula**: `Ek = (1/2)mv²`
>
> `Ek` = Kinetic Energy (J)
>
> `m` = Mass (kg)
>
> `v` = Velocity (m/s²)

---

### Gravitational Potential Energy

Gravitational Potential Energy comes with **height**.

**Unit**: `J`

> **Formula**: `Ep = mgh`
>
> `Ep` = Gravitational Potential Energy (J)
>
> `m` = Mass (kg)
>
> `g` = Gravity (m/s²)
>
> `h` = Height (m)

---

### Conservation of Energy

Energy **cannot be built or destroyed**, but can change from **one form to another form**.
